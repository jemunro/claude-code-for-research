# Claude Code talk for the Bahlo lab

A talk + worked-example repo for the lab meeting on 2026-05-12.
Twin purpose: deliver the talk itself, and serve as a starting
point that lab members can fork after the talk.

## Source of truth

- `OUTLINE.md` — talk content and scope. Don't drift from it
  without checking with me first.
- `REFERENCES.md` — full reference notes; superset of what ends
  up in the talk.
- `README.md` — the post-talk on-ramp, doubles as documentation
  of the conventions in this repo.
- `ACTIVITIES.md` — a list of possible activities to work on during the session
- `session_logs/YYYY-MM-DD_<topic>.md` — what we worked on, in
  date order. Read the most recent one at session start.

## Working notes

- When I ask for the deck, use `/beautiful-deck-quarto`. Source
  material is `OUTLINE.md`; figures live in `figures/`.
- The talk's goal is to show my colleagues ways of improving research workflows with  AI and what is possible
- The `README.md` treats this repo as its own worked example.
  If you change a convention in the README, check the repo
  actually follows it.
- Audience is a mix of PhD students, statistical genetics researchers and research assistants, who have a broad range of experience with software development.
